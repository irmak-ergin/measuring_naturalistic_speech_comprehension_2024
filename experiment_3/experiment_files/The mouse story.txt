The mice on the farm were happy. There was a cat, but he was very old. He did not chase mice anymore. Most days, he just lay in the sun. "Catching mice is too much work," he would say. "I would rather sleep." 


Then everything changed. One morning, the farmer came carrying a strange wooden box. He set it down slowly. The mice froze as the lid creaked open… and out stepped a sleek, shadow-black cat with eyes like burning yellow fire and claws sharp as knives.


She looked around and sniffed the air."She won't cause a problem," said a young mouse. "Just another cat. Maybe quiet and slow. The old mouse nodded, staying quiet. This cat was not the same. She was young. She moved fast. She noticed things. And worst of all... she looked hungry. 


That night, the new cat prowled the farmyard like a ghost. Her black coat melted into the shadows as she moved without a sound, waiting, listening. At dawn, the mice gathered in the barn, whispers and scared looking replacing their usual chatter. 


There were fewer mice now. "Where did they go?" the young mouse asked, looking around. The old mouse looked toward the farmhouse. The new cat sat by the door, licking her paws. Her belly was rounder. She looked satisfied. Each night, more mice vanished.


Fear clenched the mice like cold iron. A secret meeting was summoned. "We must fight back," declared the old mouse. "If we don't stop her, none of us will be left." He sighed deeply. "The trouble is, we can't hear her coming. She's silent, invisible... until it's too late.” 


"I have an idea," said the young mouse. "Lets put a bell on her collar. Then we'll hear her coming." The mice looked at each other. "That could work, " said one quietly. The others nodded. It seemed like a good, simple plan.


They scrambled into action. A group discovered a small bell, rusted but usable. Another brought string from a scarecrow's trousers. "Perfect, " the young mouse bellowed. "Tonight, we end this. No more mice will disappear. " The barn erupted in cheers. 


While the mice were talking, the old mouse spoke in a quiet voice. "There is one more thing," he said. "Who will put the bell on the cat?" The mice stopped talking. No one said anything. "It is easy to think of ideas," he said. "Doing them can be harder."
