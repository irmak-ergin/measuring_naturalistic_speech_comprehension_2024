The sun rose slowly over the snow. The trees were still, with snow resting on their branches. The air was cold and quiet. Everything stayed calm, just like the day before. 


A distant howl of a wolf echoed in the valley, but it faded into silence. Suddenly, the waves erupted, and a strange light flickered across the horizon, casting eerie shadows over the frozen landscape. 


"The two men sat on their boat and looked up. 'The wind and sun are here, ' one of them said. 'Let's hope the weather stays nice today. '" 


Suddenly, the Wind howled, ripping through the stillness like a jagged blade. "Move, Sun!" it screamed. "I will scatter your peace and tear the world asunder!" The Wind's fury broke the calm with an intensity that seemed to shake the very air. 


The wind blew strongly over the sea. The waves moved higher, and the boats shifted in the water. The sailors held on tightly as their ships moved with the wind. The sun stayed in the sky. 


Unbothered, it cast a steady gaze over the chaos below. Smirking, the sun pronounced "Let's see who's truly stronger. whoever gets that man there to remove his coat wins. " The Wind scoffed "Accepted. I'll blow him into submission. " 


A strong wind began to blow. The trees bent and birds flew away. The man pulled his coat closer and leaned forward as he walked into the wind. But despite the Wind's strength, the man did not let go of his coat. 


He yanked the coat tighter, burying himself in its fragile shelter of fabric and hope. His face, raw and windburned, held a quiet defiance that seemed to mock the storm's endless rage.. 


The Sun stayed in the sky, shining quietly. Its warmth was soft and steady. It didn't say anything. The Wind moved quickly, but the Sun stayed the same. It knew things would change in time. 


Drained from its relentless onslaught, the Wind faltered in bitter disbelief, realizing that no matter how violently it screamed, the man stood unmoved—unyielding as stone beneath a storm. 


The wind grew quiet and the Sun smiled, watching the man slowly begin to take off his coat as he warmed up. It had won. The world was still again, calm and bright under the Sun's gentle light.
