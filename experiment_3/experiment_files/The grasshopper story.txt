It was a sunny day. The Grasshopper sat on the grass. ‘What a nice day,’ he said. ‘The sun is shining, and there's lots of grass to eat’.


The Grasshopper gobbled leaf after leaf, stuffing himself until he collapsed backward, wheezing. “That’s plenty!” he groaned, clutching his full belly. “Now, time for a little concert.”


He rubbed his legs and made a quiet buzzing sound. “Sounds good,” he said. “Perfect for a sunny day.” The sound drifted gently through the air.


“Enough!” snapped an Ant, hauling a corn kernel twice his size. “Some of us are working!” The Grasshopper didn’t even blink. He grinned—wide and wild, drunk in summer. “What, you don’t like music?”


“I don’t,” said the Ant. “I’m busy.” The Grasshopper blinked. “You’re working now?” The Ant nodded. “Just getting food,” he said.

The Grasshopper watched as the Ant dragged a massive ear of corn across the dirt. “That looks exhausting,” he said. “Yeah, it’s hard,” the Ant panted.

"Why are you doing it?" asked the Grasshopper. "It’s for winter," the Ant said. "We need food later so we get it now " The Grasshopper smiled. "But winter is far away!" “Why worry?" 


The Grasshopper scoffed. "Summer's here! We grasshoppers eat, relax, and make music. It's a great life. " The Ant frowned. "Enjoy it, while it lasts, " he muttered, walking off.


"Stay a bit, " the Grasshopper said. "Let's enjoy the sun" The Ant shook his head. "I have work." He carried the corn across the field, leaving the Grasshopper alone.


The Grasshopper smirked. "Silly Ant, wasting such a beautiful day! I have sunshine, food, and music!" He laughed, rubbing his legs together, filling the air with his buzzing tune.


The days got colder. The Grasshopper stayed in one place, feeling cold and hungry. There was not much food. The fields were empty. Then he saw the Ant.


“Hey, bestie,” he whispered, trembling like a leaf caught in a storm. “Do you recall who I am?” The Ant’s eyes flickered once, then nodded slowly. “Chill bites deep tonight, doesn’t it?” The Grasshopper gave a sharp nod.

“We have food,” said the Ant. “We saved it.” The Grasshopper looked at him. “Could you share? Just a little?” He looked not just hungry—but hopeful.


He stood still, gaze locked, as if waiting for warmth in the Ant’s voice. Minutes passed like hours. “You once called me foolish,” said the Ant. “I didn’t mean it!” the Grasshopper blurted. “Just a joke!” The Ant didn’t smile.


“If you save some food now, you won’t be hungry later when it’s cold.” The Ant said. The Grasshopper spent the whole summer singing and playing but didn’t save any food. Now, when winter came, he had nothing to eat.
